Geography

Indian Geography

1. Overview of Indian Geography

India is a vast country with diverse geographical features like mountains, deserts, and coastlines.

2. The Himalayan Mountain Range

The Himalayas are the world’s highest mountain range with peaks like Everest and Kanchenjunga.

3. The Indo-Gangetic Plains

The Indo-Gangetic plains are formed by the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Indus rivers, supporting agriculture.

4. The Deccan Plateau

The Deccan Plateau covers southern India, rich in minerals, with rivers like Godavari and Krishna.

5. The Thar Desert

The Thar Desert, located in Rajasthan, has unique wildlife and a rich cultural heritage.

6. Coastal Regions

India has a 7,500 km coastline along the Arabian Sea and Bay of Bengal.

7. The Islands of India

India’s Andaman & Nicobar and Lakshadweep islands are famous for their tropical ecosystems.

8. The River Systems of India

India’s rivers like the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Indus are essential for agriculture and economy.

9. The Western and Eastern Ghats

The Western Ghats run parallel to the western coast, rich in biodiversity. The Eastern Ghats are lower in elevation and stretch along India’s eastern coast.

10. The Climate of India

India experiences tropical monsoons and temperate climates, with most rainfall between June-September.
