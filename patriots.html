Patriots

Mahatma Gandhi

Known as the "Father of the Nation," Mahatma Gandhi led India's fight for independence through nonviolence (Satyagraha). Key movements include the Salt March (1930), the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920–1922), and the Quit India Movement (1942). His philosophy inspired global leaders like Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela.

Gandhi believed in truth, nonviolence, and equality. His legacy continues to guide social and political movements worldwide.

Subhash Chandra Bose

Subhas Chandra Bose, also known as "Netaji," was a prominent leader who founded the **Indian National Army (INA)**. His motto, "Give me blood, and I will give you freedom," inspired millions to join the fight for independence.

Bose’s leadership in the armed struggle against British rule, despite differences with Mahatma Gandhi, marked him as a fierce nationalist. His disappearance in 1945 remains shrouded in mystery.

Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh, a revolutionary socialist, became a symbol of fearless patriotism. Known for his bold actions, such as the bombing of the Central Legislative Assembly in 1929, he used his trial as a platform to promote revolutionary ideas.

His famous quote, "They may kill me, but they cannot kill my ideas," captures his indomitable spirit. Bhagat Singh was martyred at the age of 23.
